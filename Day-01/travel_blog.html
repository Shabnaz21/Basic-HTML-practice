Unveiling the Unexplored: My Captivating Journey Through Bangladesh

From the bustling capital of Dhaka, filled with colorful rickshaws and vibrant markets, to the serenity of Srimangal's tea estates, Bangladesh is a country of striking contrasts. Exploring the ancient temples of Paharpur and Somapura Mahavihara, I was in awe of their rich history. The true gem, however, was the Sundarbans - a UNESCO World Heritage site. Cruising the labyrinth of mangrove-lined waterways, I was lucky to spot a Royal Bengal Tiger. The flavors of local Bengali cuisine lingered on my tongue, while the warm hospitality of the locals touched my heart. Bangladesh is a destination that offers untold adventures for the keen traveler.


What I Bought?
  1. Traditional Jamdani Sari:An intricate blend of fine muslin and cotton, displaying the prowess of local weavers.
  2. Nakshi Kantha:A quilt embroidered with folk motifs, telling tales of rural life.
  3. Pottery from Dhamrai:Handmade, reflecting the country's deep-rooted artisan traditions.
  4. Local spices and tea:Bangladesh's renowned for its diverse and aromatic culinary offerings.
  5. Jute products:From bags to mats, representing sustainable fashion and lifestyle.

My favorite food?

  • Pitha:A traditional sweet treat, varied in preparation across regions but always delightfully satisfying. The Bhapa Pitha, steamed and filled with coconut and jaggery, was particularly memorable.
  • Hilsa Fish Curry (Ilish): A national favorite, this succulent herring, cooked in mustard sauce, was a delightful explosion of flavors.
  • Biryani: The Dhakai variant of this iconic dish, loaded with tender meat, fragrant rice, and a melange of spices, left me craving more.
  • Fuchka (Pani Puri) A street-food sensation, these crispy hollow dough balls filled with tangy tamarind water, chili, chaat masala, potato, and chickpeas were a burst of flavors.
  • Mishti DoiThis sweetened yogurt was the perfect, cooling end to a spicy meal. The rich, creamy texture and sweet tang lingered long after the last bite.
